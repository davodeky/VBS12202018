Sunday's Sermon

Bible Verse
Matthew 25:1-13
  • God is about to pour on us an annointing.
  • This parable speaks about the church, not sinners out there.
  • As a body of Christ we are the bride, as individuals we are the virgins.
  • Just like the virgins, outwardly we all claim to be upright.
  • We are like oil in the deep fryer that needs to be filtered and refilled.
  • The question that our Lord seems to be asking us: is our oil clear and filtered?
  • The oil can dry up and leave a trace of it.
  • Some of us have become to nump, that we don't feel God anymore.
  • Like the virgins, we are all waiting.
  • Before the bell rang, we could not distinguish the foolish from the wise.
  • Likewise, we can not distinguish the genuine from the fake in the body of Christ today.
  • The genuine is the miraculous.
  • The fake are those that come to church hoping that that only is enough.
  • The virgins had to trim their lamps because they had been burning the entire night.
  • Why didn't the virgins share their oil?
  • God is telling us one thing: salvation is an individual business.
  • Salvation can not be shared.
  • Likewise, the race to cross into the new year is an individual one.
  • The ready virgins are the ones that went in with The Groom.
  • Why didn't the foolish virgins stick with the ones that had oil? The lamp could have been bright enough for all of them.
  • As 2018 ends, if your oil is running out, if your oil if not filtered, let's look at look around for those that have the oil and follow them.

This week

Bible Study Questions

  • Why did Jesus choose to use a parable?
  • The five wise virgins could not share their oil with the foolish. Is it possible for us to “share” our “good works of the Spirit” with others?
  • What was the final warning of Jesus and how does it apply to us today (Matthew 25:13)?
